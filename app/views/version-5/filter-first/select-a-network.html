What monitoring networks are available?
Automatic Urban and Rural network (AURN)
The AURN is the largest air pollution monitoring network in the UK.

AURN monitoring sites provide high resolution, hourly pollution data for nitrogen dioxide, PM10, PM2.5, ozone and sulphur dioxide.

You can use data from the AURN to check compliance with the Ambient Air Quality Directive. This directive sets air quality standards for the UK.

Locally-managed automatic monitoring network
Most locally managed air pollution monitoring sites are run by local authorities.

It provides air pollution data from across the UK, but the government do not use this data in their national monitoring.

This network may include data from other air quality monitoring programmes, industry and airports.
